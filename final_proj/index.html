CS 184 - Final Project

Title: No rest for the liquid

Team Members: David Ban, Jackie Dai, Hailey Tran

Summary

This project aims to create a video game that will be able to realistically render water that will react to various forces over time. The player will be able to place objects that can then interact with the water in a three dimensional way.

Problem Description

Fluid and particle simulation is a difficult challenge in computer graphics because of its complex nature of interacting between many objects. In order to achieve a form of realism, many different physical constraints must be considered. However, many of the previous projects only tackle this through a static video render. This project will hope to deliver an interactable form of fluid render through a video game.

There will be quite a few hurdles that we must jump through before submitting our deliverables. This includes fully deciding what other game mechanics that we will implement in our game, along with working in a game engine, which was not touched on in class. We will have to navigate a new environment that may take much more time than we expect.

Goals and Deliverables

What we plan to deliver

Our baseline goal is to include a somewhat realistic water / particle simulator that can mimic the movement of water in real time within a video game. The specific game mechanics are currently undecided, but the ideal scenario would be where any student is able to create and interact with the fluid simulator rather than just looking at a video.

What we hope to deliver

We hope to deliver a full game that a user can download and play without needing a super beefy computer, while still having a fully realistic particle simulator. We would also hope to add more customizability in what the player can control (including things such as viscosity and density of liquid), and how fast it would take to render.

Quality and Performance

Performance will be measured through the game’s capability of rendering real-time realistic particle fluid renders within a game engine. Although it is not entirely possible to set precise target metrics, the ability to render a video within a minute of the player finishing their “build” would be ideal.
